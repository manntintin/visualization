# Visualization 2 Description (Excel)

## What software did you use to create your data visualization?

I used **Microsoft Excel** to make this horizontal bar chart. Excel is easy to use and many people know how to use it. It’s good for quick data visual and good for people who are not tech-savvy.

## Who is your intended audience?

This chart is made for the **general public**, especially people in Hong Kong or Toronto who may not know coding but want to understand how short-term rental numbers are spread across different areas.

## What information or message are you trying to convey with your visualization?

The chart shows the **total number of short-term rental listings** in April 2025 for six areas – including Canada, Ontario, and four smaller Ontario regions. It lets people see which areas have more or fewer rentals.

## What aspects of design did you consider when making your visualization? How did you apply them? With what elements of your plots?

- **Chart type**: I used horizontal bar chart because some region names are long.
- **Color**: I used a simple blue color to keep the chart clean.
- **Labels**: I put numbers directly on the bars to make it easy to read.
- **Font and layout**: I kept everything clear and easy to read.

## How did you ensure that your data visualizations are reproducible? If the tool you used to make your data visualization is not reproducible, how will this impact your data visualization?

Even though Excel is not like Python (no code), I kept the steps simple and clear. Anyone can follow and make the same chart again easily.

## How did you ensure that your data visualization is accessible?

- I used text labels on bars so people don’t rely on color.
- Horizontal layout is easy to read.
- I exported it as a clear image so it can be used in reports or shared with others.

## Who are the individuals and communities who might be impacted by your visualization?

- **Government** workers or planners in Ontario
- **Housing groups** who care about how many rentals are in different areas
- **People living in those communities** who are affected by short-term rentals

## How did you choose which features of your chosen dataset to include or exclude from your visualization?

I only used the "total listing count" from April 2025 for six regions. I didn’t include things like occupancy rate or revenue, so the chart is focused and easy to understand.

## What ‘underwater labour’ contributed to your final data visualization product?

- Finding the right data inside a messy Excel file
- Cleaning up and re-typing the numbers
- Making the chart in Excel and checking layout and font
- Exporting the chart image to use in the project
