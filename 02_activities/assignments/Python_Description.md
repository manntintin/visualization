# Visualization 1 Description (Python)

## What software did you use to create your data visualization?
I used **Python** and libraries like **pandas**, **matplotlib**, and **seaborn** to make this bar chart. These tools are good for cleaning data and drawing charts. They are flexible and you can repeat the same steps to get the same result.

## Who is your intended audience?
This chart is for the **general public**, like Toronto people or anyone who want to know where the short-term rentals are located in the city.

## What information or message are you trying to convey with your visualization?
This chart shows the **number of short-term rental listings** in different areas of Toronto and Ontario in April 2025. It helps people see which areas have more short-term rentals. This can relate to tourism, housing problems, or places that need more policy control.

## What aspects of design did you consider when making your visualization? How did you apply them? With what elements of your plots?
- **Color**: I used `viridis` color palette because it's friendly to people with color blindness.
- **Sorting**: I sorted the regions from most to least listings to make it easier to compare.
- **Font and layout**: I made sure the titles and labels are clear and easy to read.
- **Spacing**: The chart is neat and not too crowded.

## How did you ensure that your data visualizations are reproducible? If the tool you used to make your data visualization is not reproducible, how will this impact your data visualization?
I wrote all the steps in Python code, including reading the file, cleaning data, and drawing the chart. As long as someone has the same Excel file, they can run the code and get the same chart.

## How did you ensure that your data visualization is accessible?
- I used color that is friendly to colorblind users.
- Fonts are big enough to read easily.
- I added labels and sorted the data so people don’t need to rely on color only.
- The final chart is saved in high-quality PNG, good for reports and screen readers.

## Who are the individuals and communities who might be impacted by your visualization?
- **Local people** who care about housing in their area.
- **Government or city planners** who want to manage short-term rental rules.
- **Tourism industry** who want to see where people rent places.

## How did you choose which features of your chosen dataset to include or exclude from your visualization?
I focused on "Total Listings Count" in April 2025 by region. I didn’t include other data like occupancy or revenue, to keep the chart simple and easy to understand for everyone.

- Figuring out how to read and clean the Excel file
